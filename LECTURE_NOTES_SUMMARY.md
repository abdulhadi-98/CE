## CORE FRAMEWORKS COVERED

1. **Three-Dimensional Innovation Framework**
   - Product/Service, Process, Marketing dimensions
   - Incremental to Radical scale
   - Business Model Innovation integration

2. **Innovation Intensity Model**
   - Two-dimensional: Degree × Frequency
   - Three-dimensional: Adding Speed
   - Strategic goal: Maximize all dimensions

3. **Innovation Risk Framework**
   - Innovation scale × Market newness
   - Core competence distance assessment

4. **Service-Dominant Logic**
   - Transformation from products to services
   - All industries as service industries

5. **Blue Ocean Strategy**
   - Uncontested market space creation
   - Dominant logic challenge

6. **Florida's 3-T Model**
   - Technology + Talent + Tolerance
   - Geographic innovation clusters

7. **Three-Player Game (Janeway)**
   - State + Financial + Private sector roles
   - Innovation ecosystem dynamics

## MAJOR THEMES EXPLORED

### 1. **Innovation Definition Challenge**
- Multiple dimensions and types
- Measurement difficulties
- Incremental vs. radical innovation
- Business model innovation importance

### 2. **The Innovation Paradox**
- Innovation is risky
- But not innovating is riskier
- Portfolio approach to balance risk-return

### 3. **Public-Private Innovation Dynamic**
- Government funds early-stage/basic research
- Private sector commercializes
- Debate over credit and value creation

### 4. **Dominant Logic Trap**
- Success creates mental models
- Blinds organizations to disruption
- Microsoft, Kodak, Blockbuster examples
- Need for organizational ambidexterity

### 5. **Size Matters (But Not How You Think)**
- Large firms: Resource advantages
- Small firms: Behavioral advantages
- Context determines advantage
- Collaboration creates synergies

### 6. **Evidence Inconclusive**
- Innovation-profitability link unclear
- Measurement challenges
- Lagged effects
- Causation vs. correlation issues

## CASE STUDY HIGHLIGHTS

### Rolls-Royce TotalCare® (Most Comprehensive)
- **Innovation Type**: Business model transformation
- **Change**: Product sale → Service provision
- **Result**: Power-by-the-hour model dominates industry
- **Impact**: Environmental + economic benefits aligned
- **Strategic Lesson**: Service-dominant logic in action

### Microsoft Missing Internet
- **Innovation Type**: Failure to exploit radical opportunity
- **Cause**: Dominant logic protecting existing revenue
- **Result**: Lost search engine market to Google
- **Strategic Lesson**: Recognition ≠ Action

### Swatch
- **Innovation Type**: Market paradigm shift through design
- **Change**: Watches as fashion accessories vs. timekeeping tools
- **Result**: Created new market, repeat purchase behavior
- **Strategic Lesson**: Design as strategic differentiator
